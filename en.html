Authentic Skills and Sensibility in Every Gentle Dish

Head Chef
Lee Migyeong

At the heart of Korian's flavors is chef Lee Migyeong, a native of Korea.
She came to Japan in 2003 and spent 10 years as head chef at the Korean restaurant "Koga" in the main Isetan Shinjuku store in Tokyo.
During her tenure, she earned a reputation as the top Korean restaurant in the Shinjuku Sanchome area, gaining support from the media and food connoisseurs for her skills and flavors.

Chef Lee's cuisine is "Japanese-Korean creative cuisine," combining traditional Korean wisdom and techniques with the sensibilities and seasons of Japan.
She pursues both "health" and "deliciousness" with dishes such as herbal cuisine using ginseng, jujube, and chestnuts, ingredients sourced directly from Korea, and homemade fermented seasonings.

Her approach to cooking is always sincere: "Food made with someone in mind is what heals both body and soul," says Chef Lee.
Her style of infusing every dish with care for the person who will eat it remains unchanged from her Isetan days and continues to thrive in the Korian kitchen.

"Flavors you never get tired of, dishes you can eat with peace of mind."
Rather than showiness, it's the careful accumulation of effort that creates food you'll want to enjoy every day.
As a professional and a lover of cooking, Lee Migyeong stands in the kitchen today with all her heart.

Korian's Commitment

Natural flavors that soak into your body.

At Korian, we do not rely on chemical seasonings. Instead, we use homemade fermented seasonings prepared over time to bring out the natural flavors of our ingredients.

We carefully recreate gentle, body-friendly tastes based on the principle of "food as medicine," blending Korean tradition with Japanese sensibility.

Please enjoy the natural umami, power of fermentation, and harmonious flavors with all your senses.
